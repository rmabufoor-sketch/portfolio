About Me

From the bedside to the bench

I began my career as a licensed Respiratory Therapist, managing ventilator support and critical respiratory care for patients across intensive care units. That clinical grounding gave me a firsthand understanding of how disease actually manifests in the human body — and it's what eventually pulled me toward the genomic mechanisms driving those conditions.

Today I work as a Genomic Epidemiologist, designing and validating bioinformatics pipelines that turn raw sequencing reads into actionable epidemiological insight. I'm currently a candidate for a Master of Science in Bioscience, building on the analytical foundation I established during my Master of Public Health, where I graduated with a 3.8 GPA and served as Student Senate President.

I care deeply about reproducibility — every pipeline I validate is version-controlled, documented, and built so the next researcher can trust the output without having to re-derive it themselves.

Featured Project

Genomic Pipeline Validation for Brain Tissue Analysis

Designed and validated an end-to-end RNA-seq quantification pipeline for post-mortem brain tissue samples, benchmarking Salmon-based transcript abundance estimates against alignment-based methods to ensure reproducibility across neurodegenerative disease cohorts. The validated pipeline reduced quantification discrepancies between replicate runs and established a version-controlled, documented workflow now used as the standard for downstream differential expression analysis.
